## PIRACY LABELS

### PRIMARY LABELS (Distribution Strategies)
Exactly one primary label must be assigned for each piracy post.

### Direct Download
Posts that distribute pirated media directly as files or attachments within the platform, without relying on external http/https links as the main delivery path.

### Channel/Bot Routing
Posts that route users to channels, bots, or in-platform paths to obtain pirated content (internal or Telegram-centric distribution rather than generic web links).

### Streaming/Magnet Links
Posts that use unauthorized streaming entry points, torrents, magnet links, or similar peer-to-peer / streaming mechanisms to access pirated content.

### Cloud Storage Links
Posts that link to pirated files hosted on cloud storage, file hosting, or comparable external file-sharing endpoints.

### SECONDARY LABELS (Operational and Ecosystem Signals)
Assign secondary labels when these operational or ecosystem signals are salient in the post. 

### Dedicated Content Channels
Posts that promote or rely on dedicated channels whose main role is hosting or distributing pirated material.

### Directory/Index Channels
Posts that point to directory, index, or aggregator channels that catalog or list piracy sources.

### Backup Channels
Posts advertising backup channels, mirror sites, or alternate destinations after bans, takedowns, or link removals.

### Intermediary Routing
Posts that use intermediate hops, forwarding, or routing layers between the user and the pirated content.

### VPNs/Proxies/Mirrors
Posts that emphasize VPNs, proxies, mirrors, DNS or circumvention-style access to reach pirated sources or blocked endpoints.

### Modded Streaming Apps
Posts promoting or distributing modified streaming applications or players used to access pirated streams.

### Streaming Credentials
Posts offering, sharing, or trading credentials, shared accounts, or unauthorized access to paid streaming services.

### Access Tutorials
Posts with step-by-step instructions to stream, download, or unlock pirated content or tools.

### Content Delivery Bots
Bots whose primary role is delivering pirated files or streams to users.

### Dynamic Retrieval Bots
Bots that fetch, resolve, or assemble content on demand rather than static file drops.

### Channel Promotion Bots
Bots used mainly to advertise channels, groups, or directories for piracy.

### Content Ingestion Bots
Bots or automation described as ingesting, mirroring, or reposting pirated content from other sources.

### Content Requests
Posts where users ask for specific pirated titles or providers fulfill such requests.

### Channel Referrals
Posts that recruit users to join channels, groups, or invite-only piracy communities.

### Forced Join
Posts or flows that coerce or strongly pressure users into joining channels or groups to access content.

### Credit Purchase
Posts where access depends on buying credits, vouchers, or similar paid tokens for pirated content or bots.

### Premium Quality Tiers
Posts selling higher tiers of quality, bitrate, or exclusivity for pirated content.

### Incentivized User Uploads
Posts that reward or incentivize users to upload, seed, or contribute pirated material.

### Resolution/Encoding
Posts explicitly highlighting resolution, codecs, encodes, or release-quality tags (e.g., 1080p, x265, WEB-DL).

### Bundled Collections
Posts offering packs, seasons, bulk archives, or bundled sets of pirated media.

### Subtitles/Dubs
Posts emphasizing subtitles, dubs, language tracks, or localization of pirated content.
